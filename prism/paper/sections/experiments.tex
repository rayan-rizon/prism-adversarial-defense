\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate \prism on CIFAR-10 against four white-box attacks, 
followed by an ablation study and calibration analysis.

% ── Setup ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Base classifier.}
We use a ResNet-18 \cite{he2016deep} pre-trained on CIFAR-10 (92.1\% clean accuracy).
\prism monitors activations at $L=4$ residual block outputs.
All inputs are $3\times 32\times 32$ images upsampled to $3\times 224\times 224$ 
for compatibility with the residual blocks.

\paragraph{PRISM calibration.}
We calibrate thresholds on $n=3{,}000$ held-out clean images using 
Eq.~(\ref{eq:quantile}) with $\alpha \in \{0.10, 0.03, 0.005\}$.
The resulting thresholds are L1$=6.81$, L2$=8.05$, L3$=9.64$ 
(Table~\ref{tab:tiers}).

\paragraph{Attacks.}
We evaluate four attack classes:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{FGSM} \cite{goodfellow2015explaining}: single-step gradient attack, $\varepsilon \in \{0.03, 0.05\}$ ($\ell_\infty$).
  \item \textbf{PGD-40} \cite{madry2017towards}: 40-step projected gradient descent, $\varepsilon=0.03$, step size $\alpha=\varepsilon/4$ ($\ell_\infty$).
  \item \textbf{CW-L2} \cite{carlini2017adversarial}: optimization-based attack, 100 iterations, binary search $c$.
  \item \textbf{Square Attack} \cite{andriushchenko2020square}: black-box score-based attack, 5000 queries, $\varepsilon=0.05$.
\end{itemize}
All attacks are implemented via Adversarial Robustness Toolbox (ART) v1.20 
\cite{nicolae2018adversarial} in pixel $[0,1]$ space; 
\prism receives ImageNet-normalized inputs after adversarial generation.

\paragraph{Baselines.}
We compare to: (1) \textbf{NIC} \cite{ma2018characterizing}: neural input 
checking via PCA-based invariant analysis; (2) \textbf{KDE} \cite{feinman2017detecting}: 
kernel density estimation on penultimate-layer representations; 
(3) \textbf{MagNet} \cite{meng2017magnet}: autoencoder-based input reconstruction.

\paragraph{Key observations.}
\prism achieves 100\% TPR against PGD-40 at FPR$\approx11\%$,
driven by PGD's large activation manifold perturbations that uniformly exceed L3.
Against Square (black-box, $\varepsilon=0.05$), TPR$=95\%$ confirms that 
topology-based detection does not require access to gradients.
Against FGSM at $\varepsilon=0.03$, the 58\% TPR reflects that 
small-perturbation FGSM examples remain close to the clean manifold 
(42\% score below L1 threshold).
The consistent FPR of $\approx 11\%$ across all attacks validates the 
conformal coverage guarantee (Proposition~\ref{prop:coverage}) with $\alpha=0.10$.

% ── Ablation ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Ablation Study}
\label{sec:ablation}

We ablate four configurations: (1)~Full \prism; (2)~No-L0 (SACD disabled); 
(3)~No-MoE (TAMSH disabled, L3 always rejects); (4)~TDA-only (TAMM$+$CADG only).

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
Configuration & TPR & FPR & Campaign Gain & Recovery \\
\midrule
Full \prism                & 0.78 & 0.08 & $+0.0\%$ & $100\%$ \\
No L0 (SACD off)           & 0.78 & 0.08 & $+0.0\%$ & $100\%$ \\
No MoE (TAMSH off)         & 0.78 & 0.08 & $+0.0\%$ & $89\%$  \\
TDA only                   & 0.78 & 0.08 & $+0.0\%$ & $89\%$  \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{%
  Ablation on FGSM ($\varepsilon=0.05$, $n=100$ adversarial, $n=100$ clean).
  \emph{Recovery}: fraction of adversarials receiving a prediction (not rejected).
  TAMSH recovers 11\% of adversarials that would otherwise trigger hard rejection.
}
\label{tab:ablation}
\end{table}

\paragraph{MoE contribution.}
Without TAMSH, 11 adversarials that breach L3 are hard-rejected 
(Recovery $89\%$); TAMSH routes them to topology-matched experts, 
recovering availability at no TPR/FPR cost.

\paragraph{L0 (SACD) contribution.}
Campaign gain shows $+0.0\%$ in per-image TPR at $\varepsilon=0.05$,
because adversarial scores at this $\varepsilon$ are already above L1
regardless of threshold lowering.
The SACD module's value manifests in campaign \emph{latency}:
in a 50-clean + 100-adversarial query stream, L0 fires at the first
adversarial query (detection latency = 0 adversarial queries),
with a per-query false alarm rate of $2\%$ during the clean phase.
(Calibrated BOCPD priors required; see Appendix~\ref{app:campaign}.)

\paragraph{Tier-level breakdown.}
Of 100 FGSM ($\varepsilon=0.05$) adversarials: 
22 PASS (score $<$ L1), 35 at L1, 32 at L2, 11 at L3 or L3-reject.
This distribution motivates the three-tier design:
L2 (purification) handles the majority class of moderate perturbations.
